1. What are bathrooms like where I stay in each stage?

> Bathrooms are a local traditional timorese style. There will be no shower but clean water in a big bucket which you can use to wash your hands and body. Hand soap will be prepared in each toilet.

 

2. How can I use water in the bucket to wash my body?

> Please use a plastic water dipper to dip up some water and do not get in the bucket to wash yourself

 

3. What sort of weather and temperatures can I expect?

> It will be about 20 - 25℃ in day time and 3 - 15℃ at night in the mountains, and 25 – 30℃ all day in Dili.

 

4. Do I need warm clothes during the tour?

> It can be cold in the mountains so we recommend you bring a fleece or light rain jacket with you.

 

5. Do I need to get vaccination before come to East Timor?

> Timor is a tropical country. Make sure you have insect repellent and that your vaccinations are up-to-date.

   If you have concerns, talk to your doctor.

 

6. What sort of food will be provided?

> Basically food is East Timorese style such as:

Breakfast: rice porridge, bread, eggs, boiled potatoes, cheese, fruits, coffee, tea, milk and fruit juice.

Lunch & Dinner: rice, potatoes, eggs, salad, beef, chicken, fruits, stir-fried vegetables, tea, coffee, and fruit juice.

 

7. Is there any power available at campsites each night for charging phones and other devices?

> Yes. Power is available 24 hours a day at each stage

 

8. When I finish a stage outside the cut-off time, will I be allowed to start the next day?

> Yes. As long as you do not have any health problem with daily morning check-ups, you can start again the next day.

 

9. What about money?

> Timor-Leste uses US Dollars. Outside Dili you need cash. There are ATMs in Dili which accept foreign cards.

   Small bills are more useful to buy food or souvenirs.
